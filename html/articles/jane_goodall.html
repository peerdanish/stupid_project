Timeline

  • 1934 : Dame Jane Morris Goodall was born on 3 April, 1934 in London to Mortimer Herbert Morris-Goodall, a businessman and Margaret Myfanwe Joseph, a novelist. She had a younger sister, Judy.
  • 1952: She left school at the age of 18 because of her love for wildlife in order to pursue her dream.
  • 1957: She was employed as a secretary in Oxford University and also worked at a London based documentary film company to finance her trip.
  • 1962: In 1962, Leakey enrolled her in Cambridge University by raising funds and she obtained a PhD degree in ethology.
  • 1977: In 1977, she established Jane Goodall Institute to protect wildlife, especially chimpanzees all over the world
  • 1995: In 1995, she was honored with National Geographic Society Hubbard Medal for Distinction in Exploration, Discovery and Research.
  • 2006: In 2006, she was honored with 60th Anniversary Medal of the UNESCO and French Legion Honor.
